⚙️ Physics & Engineering

The science behind wind power calculations and turbine design.

Small Wind Guidebook
U.S. Department of Energy, WindExchange
Comprehensive guide for homeowners on evaluating, installing, and maintaining small wind systems. Covers site assessment, economics, permitting, and grid interconnection. The authoritative US government reference for residential wind.
Small Wind Electric Systems: A U.S. Consumer's Guide
NREL / U.S. Department of Energy · PDF
Accessible, homeowner-focused guide covering how small wind works, what to expect in terms of output and cost, and how to evaluate whether your site is suitable. Published by NREL, free download.
Small Wind Site Assessment Guidelines
NREL, 2014 · PDF
Technical guidelines for assessing wind resources at specific sites, including measurement methodology, data analysis, and interpretation. Relevant for anyone considering an anemometer study before committing to a turbine installation.
NREL Small Wind Research
National Renewable Energy Laboratory
NREL's small wind program hub, including testing data, turbine certification information, and links to the National Wind Technology Center. The best source for verified performance data on certified small turbines.
Wind Power Physics: The Betz Limit & Power Curve
Core physics used in this tool
Wind power follows P = ½ρACpv³, where ρ is air density (1.225 kg/m³ at sea level), A is rotor swept area, Cp is the power coefficient (capped at 16/27 ≈ 0.593 by the Betz limit), and v is wind speed in m/s. Height correction uses the ¹⁄₇ power law: v(h) = v₁₀ × (h/10)^0.143. Power scales with the cube of wind speed — doubling the wind means 8× the power.

📚 Books & DIY Guides

The essential reading list for serious homebrewers.

Homebrew Wind Power
Dan Bartmann & Dan Fink · Buckville Publications, 2009 · ISBN 978-0981920108
The definitive English-language guide to building your own axial-flux wind turbine. Covers wind theory, generator design, blade carving, wiring, and off-grid system integration. Written by two engineers who actually live this way. Available at most libraries.
A Wind Turbine Recipe Book
Hugh Piggott · Scoraig Wind · 2008, updated editions · Free PDF available
The open-source manual that sparked a global movement. Covers 6 sizes of axial-flux wind turbines buildable with simple hand tools and mostly locally-sourced materials. Translated into 10+ languages; used by community builders from Nepal to Chile to Poland.
Wind Power Workshop: Building Your Own Wind Turbine
Hugh Piggott · Centre for Alternative Technology, 1997
Piggott's earlier, more concise manual — still widely used and recommended. A good companion to the Recipe Book for hands-on workshop situations.
The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind
William Kamkwamba & Bryan Mealer · William Morrow, 2009 · ISBN 978-0061730337
The memoir of William Kamkwamba, who at 14 built a wind turbine from scrap in rural Malawi to power his family during a famine. Not a technical manual, but one of the most compelling arguments for why small wind power matters. Also a 2019 Netflix film.
The Piggott Ferrite Designs — DIY Book
PureSelfMade / Jonathan Schreiber
Updated guide covering the newer ferrite magnet variant of the Piggott turbine, with improved documentation for modern builds. Practical companion to Piggott's Recipe Book.

🔌 Data Sources Used in This Tool

APIs and datasets that power the Windy wind calculator.

Open-Meteo Historical Weather API
Open-Meteo · Free, no API key · ERA5 reanalysis data
Windy's primary wind data source. Returns hourly wind speed and direction at 10 m height for any location on Earth going back to 1940, based on ECMWF's ERA5 reanalysis dataset. Free for non-commercial use, ~0.25° spatial resolution (~25 km). This tool caches responses in PostgreSQL to minimize API calls.
Nominatim Geocoding API
OpenStreetMap Foundation · Free, no API key
Converts street addresses and ZIP codes into latitude/longitude coordinates and returns structured address data including US state codes. Used by Windy to convert user-entered addresses into coordinates for wind data lookup.
EIA State Electricity Profiles
U.S. Energy Information Administration · Updated annually
Source for the state-average residential electricity rates (cents/kWh) embedded in this tool. Windy currently uses 2024 EIA state averages. Full data available for download; a future version will call the EIA API for live rates.
NREL Wind Integration National Dataset (WIND) Toolkit
National Renewable Energy Laboratory · Free API key required
Higher-resolution historical wind data for the continental US (2 km resolution, 5-minute intervals). Not yet integrated into Windy but planned as an optional high-accuracy mode for US locations. Requires a free API key from developer.nrel.gov.
DOE Wind Resource Maps
U.S. Department of Energy · WindExchange
National and state-level maps showing average annual wind speed at various heights. Useful for a quick visual check on whether your region has meaningful wind resources before doing a detailed analysis.

💬 Online Communities & Forums

Where serious DIY wind builders share knowledge and ask questions.

Otherpower.com — Wind Power
Dan Bartmann & Dan Fink · Colorado, USA
The web's best technical reference for homebrewed wind power. Extensive documentation on building axial-flux alternators, carving blades, winding coils, and setting up off-grid systems. Real, detailed, and based on years of actual experience.
Fieldlines.com
The Otherpower Discussion Board
The companion forum to Otherpower.com. Active community of serious DIY wind and solar builders. Deep technical threads covering every aspect of small-scale power generation, storage, and off-grid living.
DIY Solar Power Forum — Wind & Hydro Section
diysolarforum.com
Active forum covering small wind turbines alongside solar and off-grid topics. Good for troubleshooting charge controllers, inverters, and hybrid wind-solar systems.
PureSelfMade Blog & Workshop Reports
Jonathan Schreiber · Austria
Detailed workshop reports, project walkthroughs, and DIY guides from one of the most active Piggott turbine workshop organizers in the world. Each report documents a real build from start to finish with photos and technical notes.

📋 Policy, Incentives & Certification

Rules, regulations, and financial incentives that affect homeowner wind projects.

Distributed Wind Energy Association (DWEA)
US trade association for small wind · Washington, DC
Advocacy and education for distributed (small) wind power in the US. Tracks state-by-state policy on net metering, permitting, and incentives. Essential resource for understanding the regulatory landscape for a home wind project.
DSIRE — Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency
NC State University / U.S. Department of Energy
The most comprehensive source for state and federal incentive programs for renewable energy, including small wind. Search by state and technology to find tax credits, rebates, net metering policies, and loan programs that apply to your project.
Small Wind Certification Council (SWCC)
Independent certification body for small wind turbines
Provides independent, accredited certification of small wind turbines based on AWEA performance and safety standards. Certified turbines are eligible for federal tax credits. If you're buying a commercial turbine (vs. building one), check SWCC certification first.
Permits, Covenants, and Zoning for Small Wind
DOE WindExchange / Small Wind Guidebook
Overview of the local permitting landscape for small wind turbines — including typical setback requirements, height restrictions, HOA considerations, and noise ordinances. A critical first step before investing in a turbine.

ℹ️ About This Tool

Windy is an open-source homeowner tool built by Kevin Traywick. Wind data is sourced from the Open-Meteo historical API (ERA5 reanalysis). Electricity rates from the U.S. Energy Information Administration (2024 state averages). Geocoding by Nominatim / OpenStreetMap. Map tiles by OpenStreetMap contributors, rendered via Leaflet.js. Built with React, Tailwind CSS, Node.js, and PostgreSQL. Deployed on Railway.
