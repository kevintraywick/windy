Individual

Hugh Piggott

Scoraig Peninsula, NW Scotland · since 1970s

The godfather of homebrew wind power. Lives off-grid on a remote Scottish peninsula with no grid connection. Designed the globally-adopted Piggott axial-flux turbine — a hand-wound generator built from car parts, neodymium magnets, and carved wooden blades. His Wind Turbine Recipe Book has been translated into 10+ languages and sparked hundreds of community builds worldwide. Teaches hands-on courses at Scoraig and around the world.

Individual

William Kamkwamba

Wimbe, Malawi · built first turbine age 14, 2001

During a famine that forced him out of school, William built a functioning wind turbine from bicycle parts, tractor fan blades, and scrap metal — using a book he found in a library. It powered his family's home and eventually a water pump. His story became a global phenomenon through the bestselling memoir and 2019 Netflix film The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind. Now an engineer, he founded the Moving Windmills Project to support rural communities in Malawi.

Individual

Dan Bartmann & Dan Fink

Northern Colorado mountains, USA · off-grid since 1991

Two engineers living off the grid in the Colorado Rockies — 11 miles from the nearest power pole. They built their lives around homebrewed wind and solar power, documented everything, and wrote the definitive English-language guide: Homebrew Wind Power (2009). Their website Otherpower.com is the most comprehensive technical resource for DIY axial-flux turbine builders, with detailed guides on winding coils, pouring resin, and carving blades.

Jonathan Schreiber / PureSelfMade

Austria · workshops across Europe & South America · since 2013

Austrian engineer turned nonprofit workshop leader. Since 2013, Jonathan has run 3–5 day hands-on courses where participants build complete Piggott-style turbines from scratch — generator, rotor blades, metalwork, and electrics. Over 900 participants, 80+ turbines built across Poland, France, Germany, Austria, Chile, and beyond. In 2024 alone he ran ~12 workshops. Courses are open to anyone; no prior engineering experience needed.

Organization

Open Source Ecology

Kansas, USA (global) · founded 2003 · Marcin Jakubowski

Marcin Jakubowski's global network of engineers designing a free, open-source Global Village Construction Set — 50 industrial machines (including wind turbines, tractors, 3D printers) that any community can build themselves at a fraction of commercial cost. All designs are published freely. Their open-source wind turbine documentation covers vertical-axis designs buildable from stove pipe, metal brackets, and a trailer hub. A TED Talk that reached millions.

Wind Empowerment

Global · 1,000+ members · 50+ countries

A global association promoting locally-manufactured small wind turbines for rural electrification and sustainability. Rooted in the Piggott open-source design, member organizations have built turbines in Nepal, Argentina, India, Peru, Tanzania, Ethiopia, and dozens more countries. Represents wind turbine manufacturers, NGOs, universities, cooperatives, and individual practitioners. Maintains an open knowledge base and coordinates international collaboration on small wind.

Organization

Distributed Wind Energy Association

USA · small & distributed wind advocacy

The US trade and advocacy association specifically for small and distributed wind — turbines installed where people live and work, not utility-scale wind farms. The DWEA works with state and federal policymakers on permitting, net metering, tax incentives, and interconnection rules that directly affect homeowners considering wind. Publishes annual market reports and educational resources on the state of residential wind power in the US.

The most technically serious online community for DIY wind power builders. USA · since 1970 · homesteading & DIY self-sufficiency

The original homesteading publication, covering DIY wind power since the 1970s energy crisis. Their archive of wind turbine build guides, alternator conversions, and small-scale energy projects represents 50+ years of reader-contributed practical knowledge. The car alternator wind generator article is one of the web's most-replicated DIY wind guides. A cornerstone resource for Americans who want to reduce utility dependence through self-built systems.
